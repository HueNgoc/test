EVENT DAY INTRODUCING THE 'TOGETHER WITH ME PROTECTING PU MAT FOREST' CLUB WITH 80 SELECTED MEMBERS.

March 3, 2024

With over 1300 registration forms received, 80 potential students from 16 schools in Anh Son, Con Cuong, and Tuong Duong districts have been selected to join the "Together with Me Protecting Pu Mat Forest" club. The club is part of Phase 4 of the Pu Mat National Park's Nature Conservation and Wildlife Education Program, spanning from 2023 to 2025. Its aim is to enhance students' awareness of nature conservation and wildlife, provide knowledge and skills to become "Junior Advocates" spreading the message, and collaborate with the core group to implement conservation activities at their schools.

On March 1st and 3rd, 2024, the Save Vietnamâ€™s Wildlife Center collaborated with Pu Mat National Park, the Department of Education and Training in Anh Son, Con Cuong, and Tuong Duong districts to organize training sessions for the "Together with Me Protecting Pu Mat Forest" club. Thirty elementary school students and fifty middle school students participated. They worked together to establish club rules, engaged in meaningful games such as "Web of Life" - illustrating the interconnectedness of all living beings, and "Threat to Elephants" - highlighting the dangers faced by elephants and other wildlife in their natural habitat. They also visited the Natural History Museum. Additionally, club members, along with teachers, devised action plans to protect wildlife and nature at their schools. Many ideas were proposed, including mural painting, theatrical performances, promising an exciting time ahead.

"I feel very proud to be selected for the club. Wild animals are very pitiful, facing many dangers in their own home like traps set by hunters. I will spread the message of nature conservation and wildlife protection to my classmates and school." - A student from Chau Khe Elementary School shared. "As a club member, I will try to save paper, dispose of trash properly, and work together with everyone to protect the forest and its wild animals." - A student from Tam Dinh Junior High School shared.

With the enthusiastic spirit of the younger generation, it is hoped that the nature and wildlife of Pu Mat National Park will be even more effectively protected in the future, so that Pu Mat forest will continue to sing!
